Purpose of the Role

The Revenue Queue Manager owns Exotic's revenue opportunity pipeline and ensures no potential revenue is left unattended.

The role monitors, prioritizes, assigns, and tracks every revenue-generating queue — new signups, failed payments, pending payments, renewal workloads, churned customers, KYC-pending accounts, and profile-activation opportunities. The objective: maximize conversion, retention, payment recovery, and renewal performance, keeping Sales and Customer Success focused on the highest-value work.

01

Revenue Queue Ownership

Every revenue opportunity enters a queue. Every queue has an owner, an SLA, and a conversion target.

New Signup Queue

  • New signups & first impressions
  • Profile completion progress
  • First-payment conversion
All leads assigned & contacted within SLA.

Failed Payment Queue

  • Failed transactions
  • Unmatched & pending payments
  • Recovery actions initiated
Coordinate with agents to convert failures into revenue.

Renewal Queue

  • 0–3 day renewals (urgent)
  • 4–14 day renewals (warming)
  • High-value accounts prioritized
Escalate high-risk accounts before expiry.

Churned Customer Queue

  • Recently churned users
  • High-value historical customers
  • Dormant profiles
Coordinate reactivation & win-back campaigns.

KYC Queue

  • Pending verification
  • Rejected verification
  • Reverification requests
Compliance reviews completed within SLA.

Profile Activation

  • Incomplete profiles
  • Unpublished opportunities
  • Activation nudges
Convert dormant signups into published, paying profiles.
02

Queue Prioritization

A daily priority list tells Sales & CS exactly what to work first — ranked by revenue impact and time sensitivity.

PRIORITY 1 VIP renewals expiring within 48 hours
PRIORITY 2 Failed payments above $20
PRIORITY 3 New signups less than 24 hours old
PRIORITY 4 Churned users less than 30 days old

08

What Success Looks Like

Six months in, the difference is structural — not a busier team, but a system where revenue can no longer slip away unseen.

Before
  • Opportunities scattered across tools and inboxes
  • Agents decide what to work on, ad hoc
  • Revenue leakage is invisible to leadership
After
  • Every revenue opportunity enters a queue
  • Every queue has an owner and an SLA
  • Every queue has a conversion target
  • Leadership sees revenue at risk in real time
The role becomes the air-traffic controller of revenue.

No signup, renewal, failed payment, churned customer, or verification opportunity falls through the cracks. For a business like Exotic, this role would likely generate more revenue than hiring another field salesperson — because it improves conversion across the entire customer lifecycle.
